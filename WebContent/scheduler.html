SCheduler

BACKGROUND

As a part of my Principles of Software Development course, I had the opportunity to collaborate with other students in developing a web-based Java program of our choice. For our project, we created SCheduler. SCheduler is a tool USC students can use to streamline the ever-difficult class registration process.

TECHNICAL DETAILS

Using the major and the classes a student has already taken as input, the SCheduler utilizes an algorithm we designed to generate five different course plans, each mapping out a unique arrangement of the courses the student must take that will fulfill their specific graduation requirements. The student can also save their favorite course plan to reaccess it in future visits to the webpage or regenerate five more schedules if they are unhappy with the first five.

We utilized MySQL for the functionality of saving the selected course plans to the database, as well as to store course information and major requirements. All of the back-end code was written in Java, while the entirety of the front-end was written using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. We also used the Materialize CSS Framework to beautify the website.

CONCLUSION

Working with a group of other students was definitely an unfamiliar challenge at first, but it was a great experience working with my other bright colleagues and working through our issues as a team to achieve our common goal. It was also an incredibly valuable experience to collaborate with other students to create the entire documentation for this project. The SCheduler is no longer publicly available online.
